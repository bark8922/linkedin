# LinkedIn post angles — 2026-07-09

Five angles pulled from this week's threads and mapped to real Tribe work. One evergreen at the end that doesn't need the news.

---

## 1. The "agentic recruiting" hype vs. what the funnel data actually shows
**Territory:** Recruiting observed from the inside (ops/data seat)

**Hook:** "Every recruiting vendor this month is selling 'agentic AI' that posts the job, screens, scores, and schedules with no human in the loop. I sit on the dashboard that watches our actual funnel, and here's the part the pitch skips."

**Direction:** Report, don't prescribe. You built the recruiting analytics dashboard that gives the team a daily view of the candidate funnel, time-to-hire, and recruiter performance. Use it to make one concrete observation: where in the funnel a fully-automated hand-off would quietly lose good candidates (e.g. the stage where a human recruiter's judgment changes the outcome). Include the friction: you're the automation person, so you *want* this to work, and you're describing where the data makes you hesitate. End on an open question, not a verdict.

**Timely because:** Reports this week frame end-to-end recruiting automation as the 2026 story, with vendors claiming 30-50% faster time-to-hire, while only 26% of applicants trust AI to evaluate them fairly (Joveo / MSH / Korn Ferry, 2026 recruiting-trends coverage; Metaview blog, 2026).

**Format:** Plain text + one screenshot of an (anonymized) funnel view.

## 2. "AI promoted the assistant" — reported from the seat, not the trend piece
**Territory:** The EA-to-operations evolution

**Hook:** "Fortune ran a piece this month titled roughly 'AI was supposed to replace executive assistants — it promoted them instead.' My job title still says executive assistant. What I actually did last quarter was delete a $6K/month data analyst."

**Direction:** Take the widely-shared narrative and ground it in one specific build instead of agreeing with it in the abstract. The financial dashboard you built replaced Power BI + a Keboola/Snowflake pipeline + an external analyst. The point isn't "EAs are strategic now" (preachy); it's "here's the actual mechanism by which the role changed for me, and here's what I gave up / had to learn to get there." Include the friction: the parts of the old EA work that didn't go away, or the thing you underestimated.

**Timely because:** Two Fortune pieces in the last few weeks (June 22 and July 5, 2026) plus HR News coverage argue AI is raising the bar for EAs rather than replacing them; hiring data shows AI-workflow setup went from ~12% of EA reqs in 2023 to ~47% in 2026 (Stealth Agents research, 2026).

**Format:** Plain text, maybe one before/after line of the stack it replaced.

## 3. MCP crossed into the mainstream — a note from someone who was already wiring it up
**Territory:** AI and automation builds

**Hook:** "MCP is suddenly on 'every executive agenda' this month, per CIO. I've been quietly connecting Claude to Ashby, BambooHR and our own data with it for a while. The unglamorous truth: the protocol was never the hard part."

**Direction:** Report what actually took the time. You use MCP connectors day to day (Ashby, BambooHR, GitHub, the dashboards). The interesting, only-Blake-could-write-it detail is what the hype leaves out: the real work is data readiness, naming things, and trusting the output enough to act on it, not the wiring. Tie to the specific finding that data readiness is the #1 barrier for most orgs. Keep it a field note, not a lecture.

**Timely because:** CIO ("why MCP is suddenly on every executive agenda") and multiple 2026 adoption write-ups this month report 78% of enterprise AI teams have MCP-backed agents in production; SHRM's 2026 data names data readiness (47% of SMBs) as the top AI barrier.

**Format:** Plain text + a small screenshot or diagram of one connector graph.

## 4. The gap between "using AI" and "moved a pilot to production"
**Territory:** AI and automation builds (with an ops lens)

**Hook:** "The stat that stuck with me this week: worker access to AI jumped from under 40% to ~60% in a year, but only a quarter of companies moved 40%+ of their pilots into production. I've shipped a few of mine, so here's what 'into production' actually cost."

**Direction:** Use your n8n workflow fleet (expense ops, data pipelines) as the concrete example. Report the difference between a demo that works once and a workflow you trust to run unattended every day: the error handling, the edge case that broke it at 2am, the monitoring you had to add. The honest takeaway is limited: production is mostly the boring 20% nobody demos.

**Timely because:** SHRM's Navigating AI in the Workplace 2026 and enterprise reports this month highlight the adoption-to-production gap (access up to ~60%, only ~25% of firms with 40%+ of pilots in production).

**Format:** Plain text + screenshot of an n8n workflow (the messy real one, not a clean diagram).

## 5. Evergreen — the €96K/year line item that was a dashboard
**Territory:** AI and automation builds (news-independent)

**Hook:** "The recruiting analytics that used to run on Power BI plus an external data pipeline cost us about €96K a year. It's now a page that rebuilds itself every morning. Here's the one thing I'd tell anyone before they try the same swap."

**Direction:** Pure reporting from your own work — no news peg needed, always usable. Walk through one specific decision from building the recruiting dashboard (e.g. why you rebuilt the pipeline the way you did, or the metric that was wrong for weeks before you caught it). Include the cost/doubt: the swap wasn't free, something broke, or you were nervous the numbers wouldn't reconcile with the old tool. End on a limited, honest takeaway a coworker would nod at.

**Format:** Plain text + screenshot, or a short PDF carousel if you want to show the before/after stack.
